Bubble Sort

2019-11-18
#Algorithm

Bubble sort is the simplest sorting algorithm. One outer loop and one inner loop to compare and swap two adjacent elements. Every time, the outer loop completes, the biggest element will go to the end of the array.

{8,9,14,3,2,1,5}

First pass:

{8,9,14,3,2,1,5} 8<9, no need to swap

{8,9,14,3,2,1,5} 9<14, no need to swap

{8,9,3,14,2,1,5} 14>3, swap 14 & 3

{8,9,3,2,14,1,5} swap 2 & 14

{8,9,3,2,1,14,5} swap 1 & 14

{8,9,3,2,1,5,14} swap 5 & 14

So after the first pass, the biggest element 14 has been placed in the right place.

Second Pass:

{8,9,3,2,1,5,14} no need to swap

{8,3,9,2,1,5,14} swap 3 & 9

{8,3,2,9,1,5,14} swap 2 & 9

{8,3,2,1,9,5,14} swap 1 & 9

{8,3,2,1,5,9,14} swap 5 & 9

After the second pass，the second largest element–9 has been placed in the right place.

Third Pass:

{3,8,2,1,5,9,14} swap 3 & 8

{3,2,8,1,5,9,14} swap 8 & 2

{3,2,1,8,5,9,14} swap 8 & 1

{3,2,1,5,8,9,14} swap 5 & 8

After the third pass，the third largest element–8 has been placed in the right place.

Fourth Pass:

{2,3,1,5,8,9,14} swap 2 & 3

{2,1,3,5,8,9,14} swap 1 & 3

{2,1,3,5,8,9,14} no need to swap

After the fourth pass，the fourth largest element–5 has been placed in the right place.

Fifth Pass:

{1,2,3,5,8,9,14} swap 1 & 2

{1,2,3,5,8,9,14} no need to swap

After the fifth pass，the fifth largest element–3 has been placed in the right place.

Sixth Pass:

{1,2,3,5,8,9,14} no need to swap

!!! consider one more thing, why the boundary of inner loop is “j < array.length - 1 - i”?

Let’s go back to our previous example, we can see that after each element has been in its place, actually, we do not need to compare with that.

So when i = 0, inner loop will continue to j = 7- 0- 1 = 6;

when i = 1, inner loop j = 7 - i -1 = 7-1-1 =5;

…….

so when i = 5,j = 7 - 5 - 1 = 1;

Worst and Average Case Time Complexity: O(n*n). Worst case occurs when array is reverse sorted.

Best Case Time Complexity: O(n). Best case occurs when array is already sorted.
